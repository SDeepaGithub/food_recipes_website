Aloo Chapathi 🍞

Aloo Chapathi is a delicious Indian flatbread stuffed with spiced mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed to knead the dough)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (for cooking)
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
    • Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Heat a little oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add finely chopped onions, green chilies, and grated ginger. Saut√© until onions are golden brown.
    • Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
    • Add the mashed potatoes and cook for a few minutes, stirring continuously.
    • Remove from heat and let it cool. Add chopped fresh coriander leaves if desired.
  3. Stuff and Roll the Chapathis:
    • Divide the dough and the potato filling into equal portions.
    • Roll each portion of dough into a small ball and flatten it slightly.
    • Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the flattened dough.
    • Bring the edges of the dough together to seal the filling inside.
    • Gently flatten the stuffed dough ball and roll it out into a chapathi, being careful not to let the filling spill out.
  4. Cook the Chapathis:
    • Heat a tawa or skillet over medium heat.
    • Place the rolled chapathi on the hot tawa and cook until bubbles form on the surface.
    • Flip the chapathi and drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges.
    • Cook until both sides are golden brown and the chapathi is cooked through.
    • Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the hot aloo chapathis with yogurt, pickle, or your favorite curry.
