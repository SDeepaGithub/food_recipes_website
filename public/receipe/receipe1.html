Poori 🍴

Poori (also spelled Puri) is a deep-fried bread made from unleavened wheat flour dough.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Water (as needed to knead the dough)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.
    • Add 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee to the flour mixture.
    • Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a stiff, smooth dough. The dough should not be too soft or sticky.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

  2. Shape the Pooris:
    • After resting, divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball).
    • Roll each ball between your palms to make them smooth.
    • On a lightly floured surface, roll out each ball into a small circle (about 4-5 inches in diameter). The rolled dough should be even and not too thin.

  3. Heat the Oil:
    • In a deep frying pan or wok, heat enough oil for deep frying over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
    • To check if the oil is ready, drop a small piece of dough into the oil. If it rises to the surface immediately and puffs up, the oil is ready.

  4. Fry the Pooris:
    • Gently slide one rolled poori into the hot oil. It should puff up almost immediately.
    • Using a slotted spoon, gently press down on the poori to help it puff up fully.
    • Fry until the poori is golden brown on both sides. This should take about 30 seconds to 1 minute per side.
    • Remove the fried poori with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
    • Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

  5. Serve:
    • Serve the hot pooris immediately with your choice of accompaniments such as potato curry (aloo bhaji), chana masala (chickpea curry), or halwa.
